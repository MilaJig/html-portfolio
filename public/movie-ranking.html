The best movies I have ever watched

My top 3 movies of all times

The Queen's Gambit

The Queen’s Gambit is a coming-of-age drama miniseries centered on Beth Harmon, a young chess prodigy in 1950s–60s America. Orphaned at a young age, Beth discovers her gift for chess while living in an orphanage, where she also develops an addiction to tranquilizers. As she grows up, Beth battles addiction, isolation, and societal expectations while rising through the male-dominated world of competitive chess. Her journey culminates in a high-stakes showdown with the world’s top players in the Soviet Union. The series explores themes of genius, addiction, feminism, and personal growth. It’s based on the 1983 novel by Walter Tevis and stars Anya Taylor-Joy.

Hidden Figures

Hidden Figures is a 2016 film based on the true story of three African-American women—Katherine Johnson, Dorothy Vaughan, and Mary Jackson—who worked at NASA during the 1960s. Despite facing racial and gender discrimination, they played vital roles in launching astronaut John Glenn into orbit. The film highlights their brilliance, perseverance, and impact on the U.S. space program.

Frozen II

Frozen II is a 2019 animated musical fantasy film and the sequel to Disney's Frozen (2013). It follows sisters Elsa and Anna as they journey beyond Arendelle to uncover the origin of Elsa’s magical powers and save their kingdom. Alongside Kristoff, Olaf, and Sven, they explore an enchanted forest and confront the mysteries of the past. The film features themes of self-discovery, change, and courage, with popular songs like “Into the Unknown.
